## Inline equation

The lift equation is \( L = \frac{1}{2} \rho V^2 S C_L \).

The drag equation is \( D = \frac{1}{2} \rho V^2 S C_D \).

## Range equation

For a propeller aircraft, the Breguet range equation can be written as:

\[
R = \frac{\eta_p}{c} \frac{L}{D} \ln \left( \frac{W_i}{W_f} \right)
\]

where:

- \( R \) is range
- \( \eta_p \) is propeller efficiency
- \( c \) is specific fuel consumption
- \( L/D \) is lift-to-drag ratio
- \( W_i \) is initial weight
- \( W_f \) is final weight

## Battery energy range estimate

A simple electric range estimate can be written as:

\[
R = \frac{\eta_{em} e_{bat} W_{bat}}{W} \frac{L}{D}
\]

where:

- \( \eta_{em} \) is motor/propulsive efficiency
- \( e_{bat} \) is battery specific energy
- \( W_{bat} \) is battery weight
- \( W \) is aircraft weight
- \( L/D \) is lift-to-drag ratio
